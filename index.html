Edson Arantes do Nascimento "Pelé"

  • 1940 - Born in Três Corações, Brazil

  • 1956 - Joined the Santos Football Club and won nine São Paulo league championships

  • 1958 - First Brazil's world cup win, Pelé played a crucial role in Brazil's victory, scoring six goals in the tournament, including two in the final against Sweden.

  • 1962--1963 - Won both the Libertadores Cup and the Intercontinental Club Cup.

  • 1969 - in his 909th first-class match, he scored his 1,000th goal.

  • 197O - for one more World Cup tournament, he teamed with young stars Jairzinho and Rivelino to claim Brazil’s third title and permanent ownership of the Jules Rimet Trophy. Pelé finished his World Cup career having scored 12 goals in 14 games.

  • 1977 - He retired after leading the Cosmos to the league championship

  • 1978 - Pelé was the recipient of the International Peace Award

  • 1980 - Was named Athlete of the Century by the French sports publication L’Equipe

  • 2014 - The Pelé Museum opened in Santos, Brazil. In addition to his accomplishments in sports, he published several best-selling autobiographies and starred in several successful documentary and semi-documentary films. He also composed numerous musical pieces, including the soundtrack for the film Pelé (1977).

  • 2022 - Died in São Paulo, Brazil
